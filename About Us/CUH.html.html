About Central University of Haryana

The Central University of Haryana (CUH) is the only University of the state of Haryana to be funded and regulated by University Grants Commission and Ministry of Human Resource Development (MHRD), Government of India. Central University of Haryana is located at Jant-Pali villages of district Mahendergarh in South Haryana. Mahendergarh is now a part of the extended National Capital Region (NCR). The main objective of CUH is to serve as a beacon of change, through multi-disciplinary learning, for creation of knowledge community, by building a strong character and nurturing a value-based transparent work ethics, promoting creative and critical thinking for holistic development and self-sustenance for the people of India.


The University system comprises Schools equivalent to a Faculty in traditional University System, which have been defined very broadly and with wider flexibility. Each School is headed by the Dean and the Departments under the School are headed by the Head. Schools have interdisciplinary and multi-disciplinary approach with focus on both pure and applied aspects of learning. Presently, there are 30 Departments of Study, which are clubbed under 11 Schools of Study. The University offers admission in several programmes under various Schools and Departments.
